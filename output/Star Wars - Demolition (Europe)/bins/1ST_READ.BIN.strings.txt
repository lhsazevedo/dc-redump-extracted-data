TIP:
  Lasers and mounted weapons can be pre-charged to a desired stage, to be fired later in time.  To enable this feature, set Instant Fire to 'off' in the Options Menu, under Preferences.  Once enabled, hold/release R-Trigger or L-Trigger to pre-charge, then press the button again to fire!
FINAL BLOWS:
  Whenever you destroy an opponent, you have one more opportunity to please the crowd and earn some extra credits -- hit the demolished craft with either of your weapons charged to a Stage 4 attack BEFORE it blows on its own!
FORCE HITS:
  You can build up your Force Hits Meter by repeatedly hitting your opponent with charged lasers or your mounted weapon.  The meter decreases over time, or if your own craft gets hit. To unleash your accumulated Force, hit your opponent's craft with either of your weapons charged to a Stage 4 attack!
TIP:
  In Tournament and High-Stakes modes you earn credits by pummeling your opponents, but you pay for using a Recharger. To maximise your earnings, try to win extra credits by performing Force Hits and Final Blows!
TIP:
  Each weapon comes with a built-in delay to prevent overheating, so you may find it difficult to fire the same weapon in quick succession. Timing is everything!
TIP:
  Is your opponent trying to siphon some energy from a Recharger?  Use a charged Tractor Beam or a Thermal Detonator at maximum charge to kick them outta there!
TIP:
  Certain special weapons allow you to push, pull or throw your opponents.  When using these weapons, try to aim your opponent into various hazards, such as the Sarlacc Pit in the Dune Sea!
TIP:
  The Proton Torpedo is very effective at Stage 4; unfortunately it does not auto aim.  This limitation can be overcome thanks to the special weapon turrets of the AT-ST, Desert Skiff and the AAT -- just charge both weapons to the max, then slice away with the Proton Beam!
TIP:
  Experienced players may prefer to use Manual Targeting.  To enable it, set Auto Targeting to 'off' in the Options Menu, under Preferences.  Once enabled, repeatedly press the B button to manually toggle between opponents during battle.
TIP:
 The Deflector Shield Power-up causes all laser shots to deflect off of your shields!
TIP:
 The Energy Converter Power-up absorbs and recycles the energy of any laser hits striking your craft, by adding their energy to your shields and weapons supply!
TIP:
 The Cloaking Device Power-up makes your craft invisible to the opponents and their tracking weapons, but you lose the protection of your shields while cloaked!
TIP:
  Both the laser or the mounted weapon can be charged up to four stages of power by holding down R-Trigger or L-Trigger, respectively.  A Stage 4 laser attack will unleash the special weapon!
TIP:
  Each craft comes with a standard laser (R-Trigger button) and an additional slot for a mounted weapon (L-Trigger button).  Mounted weapons can be attached during battle by destroying moving Weapon Droids, then driving over the remaining red holo-cube.
TIP:
  The R2 Droid repairs your armour.  The Power Droid accelerates your shield and weapon replenishment rate.  The Tactical Droid follows you closely, blasting at the nearest opponent and accelerates your weapon charge-up time!
TIP:
  Be on the lookout for specialty droids.  While usually difficult to locate, a droid will enhance the capability of your craft for as long as you possess it.  Only three droids are available in each arena!
TIP:
  There is at least one Shield and Weapon Recharger in each arena.  Each Recharger has a limited supply of energy, represented by the brightness of the blue or red glow within it.  When all its energy is depleted, the Recharger will cease to glow.
TIP:
  Your weapons energy replenishes continuously but very slowly.  To replenish your weapons quickly, go to the nearest Weapon Recharger, marked with a red glow.  But, keep in mind that in certain game modes this will cost you credits!
TIP:
  Your shields replenish continuously but very slowly.  To replenish your shields quickly, go to the nearest Shield Recharger, marked with a blue glow.  But, keep in mind that in certain game modes this will cost you credits!
TIP:
  Your weapon energy depletes as you fire your lasers and mounted weapon. Charge-up attacks use more energy, but cause significantly more damage if the target is hit!
TIP:
  Certain weapons penetrate through shields more than others.  For instance, Concussion Missiles and Thermal Detonators penetrate more than the Proton Torpedo, but the torpedo packs more punch overall.  Also, shield penetration increases the higher the weapon charge!
TIP:
  Your shields deplete as you take damage from projectiles, explosions and collisions with other craft.  While the shields protect your craft fairly well, most projectiles will penetrate through them and cause some armour damage...
TIP:
  Do you find small and fast vehicles too difficult to hit when playing the Rancor or another slow craft?  Use the Tractor Beam to immobilise your opponent first, then unleash your lasers or, even better, your special weapon!
TIP:
  Larger vehicles have more armour, but smaller vehicles are more difficult to hit -- keep your craft on the move and use your size to your advantage!
TIP:
  If you are piloting a small craft, don't try to ram vehicles larger than yours -- you will sustain more damage than your opponent!
